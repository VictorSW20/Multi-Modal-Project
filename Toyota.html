History of Toyota

Founded in 1937 by Kiichiro Toyoda, Toyota has evolved from a modest Japanese automotive manufacturer to become one of the world's largest and most influential automakers. Toyota gained early recognition with the launch of the Toyopet Crown in 1955, marking its first venture into the international market. The innovative and fuel-efficient Corolla, introduced in 1966, became a global bestseller and solidified Toyota's reputation for reliability and practicality. The company's pioneering hybrid technology, exemplified by the Prius in 1997, further underscored Toyota's commitment to sustainability and technological advancement.

Toyota MR2 2nd Gen (I own one :D)

The Toyota MR2 SW20, produced from 1989 to 1999, is the second-generation iteration of the MR2 lineup, known for its distinctive mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive configuration. Featuring a turbocharged engine option, the SW20 delivered an engaging driving experience with its lightweight design and agile handling. Renowned for its sleek styling and sporty character, the MR2 SW20 continues to be cherished by enthusiasts as a classic representation of affordable yet spirited sports cars from the 1990s.

Toyota Supra

The Toyota Supra, introduced in 1978, is a legendary sports car that has evolved through multiple generations, earning a reputation for performance, style, and engineering excellence. Often associated with its high-performance versions, particularly the iconic twin-turbocharged models, the Supra became a symbol of Japanese automotive prowess. After a hiatus, the fifth-generation Toyota Supra was reintroduced in 2019, continuing the legacy with a turbocharged inline-six engine and a collaboration with BMW for its development, solidifying its place as a modern-day sports car icon.
