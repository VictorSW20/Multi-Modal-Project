History of Ford

Founded by Henry Ford in 1903, the Ford Motor Company revolutionized the automotive industry by introducing assembly line production techniques that significantly lowered the cost of manufacturing. The Model T, introduced in 1908, became a pioneering success as the first mass-produced car accessible to the middle class. Ford continued to innovate, introducing the first moving assembly line in 1913, further increasing efficiency and production speed. Over the years, Ford expanded its lineup to include iconic vehicles such as the Mustang, F-Series trucks, and SUVs, solidifying its position as a global automotive giant.

Ford GT

The Ford GT, initially introduced in 2005 as a tribute to the legendary GT40, is a high-performance supercar that combines cutting-edge technology with racing heritage. Powered by a twin-turbocharged V6 engine, the modern Ford GT produces over 600 horsepower, showcasing Ford's commitment to innovation and performance. With a limited production run, the GT stands as a pinnacle achievement for Ford, capturing attention for its aerodynamic design, carbon fiber construction, and its success in endurance racing, including winning its class at the prestigious 24 Hours of Le Mans.

Ford Mustang

The Ford Mustang, introduced in 1964, is an American automotive icon that revolutionized the muscle car genre with its sleek design and affordable performance. The Mustang's debut marked the birth of the "pony car" category, offering a combination of style and performance accessible to a broad market. Over the decades, the Mustang has gone through various generations, evolving with modern design and advanced technology while maintaining its status as a symbol of American muscle and driving enthusiasm.
