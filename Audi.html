History of Audi

Founded by August Horch in 1899, Audi's history is marked by mergers, including the 1932 formation of Auto Union AG with DKW, Horch, and Wanderer. Post-World War II, Audi, under Volkswagen's ownership, rebounded in the 1960s and introduced the groundbreaking Audi Quattro in 1980, revolutionizing the automotive landscape with all-wheel drive. Audi has established itself as a global luxury car leader, emphasizing technological advancements, motorsport achievements, and a commitment to sustainability, exemplified by the development of electric and hybrid vehicles in recent years. With a diverse product range and a strong global presence, Audi continues to be a prominent player in the automotive industry.

Audi R8

The Audi R8, introduced in 2006, is a distinguished mid-engine sports car renowned for its performance and design. Through two generations, it has evolved from a V8-powered model to exclusively featuring a potent V10 engine. With its distinctive styling, Quattro all-wheel-drive system, and a commitment to technological advancements, the Audi R8 has become an iconic symbol of Audi's prowess in the high-performance sports car market.

Audi Quattro

The Audi Quattro, launched in 1980, is a pioneering sports coupe recognized for introducing Audi's groundbreaking Quattro all-wheel-drive system to the automotive world. Originally developed for rallying, the Quattro system provided superior traction and stability, revolutionizing performance car dynamics. The Audi Quattro's success on the rally stage and its impact on the automotive industry cemented its legacy as a trailblazer in the realm of high-performance and all-wheel-drive vehicles.
