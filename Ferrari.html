History of Ferrari

Ferrari, founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1939 as Auto Avio Costruzioni, initially focused on producing racing cars. The first true Ferrari, the 125 S, debuted in 1947, featuring a V12 engine and establishing the marque's reputation for high-performance vehicles. Ferrari quickly became synonymous with success in motorsports, securing numerous Formula One championships and victories at prestigious races like the 24 Hours of Le Mans. Beyond racing, Ferrari expanded its lineup to include iconic road cars, like the Ferrari 250 GTO, F40, and the more recent LaFerrari, solidifying its status as a symbol of luxury, speed, and Italian craftsmanship.

Ferrari Enzo

The Ferrari Enzo, named after the company's founder, was unveiled in 2002 as a limited-production hypercar, showcasing the pinnacle of Ferrari's engineering and performance capabilities. With a mid-mounted V12 engine derived from Formula One technology, the Enzo boasted a staggering 660 horsepower and a top speed exceeding 220 mph. Limited to just 399 units, the Ferrari Enzo is highly coveted by collectors for its iconic design, technological innovation, and its significance as a tribute to the brand's founder, Enzo Ferrari.

Ferrari F40

The Ferrari F40, introduced in 1987, is an iconic supercar that stands as a symbol of performance and automotive passion. Created to celebrate Ferrari's 40th anniversary, the F40 was the last car personally approved by Enzo Ferrari before his death. With a twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 478 horsepower and a lightweight, aerodynamic design, the F40 became the first production car to break the 200 mph barrier, solidifying its status as a legendary and timeless piece of automotive history.

Ferrari Testarossa

The Ferrari Testarossa, produced from 1984 to 1996, is an iconic sports car renowned for its distinctive design and powerful performance. Featuring a mid-mounted 12-cylinder engine, the Testarossa generated 390 horsepower and could accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in under 5 seconds. Its signature side strakes and wedge-shaped profile contributed to the Testarossa's enduring status as a symbol of 1980s automotive extravagance and speed.

Ferrari SF90 stradale

Introduced in 2019, the Ferrari SF90 Stradale is a groundbreaking hybrid hypercar that combines a powerful internal combustion engine with electric motors for unparalleled performance. The SF90 Stradale is Ferrari's first series-production plug-in hybrid, featuring a twin-turbocharged V8 engine and three electric motors that deliver a combined output of 986 horsepower. With cutting-edge technology, exceptional speed, and a focus on electrification, the SF90 Stradale represents Ferrari's commitment to pushing the boundaries of performance in the modern era.
